# Exercises and Worksheets

Practical tools to strengthen your relationship.

## Love Map Exercises (爱情地图练习)

### Daily Love Map Update (5 minutes)

Each day, ask your partner:

1. What are the three most important things happening for you today?
2. What is your biggest current stressor?
3. What small thing brought you joy or frustration today?

**Action:** Listen without interrupting. Remember the details for follow-up questions later.

### Weekly Love Map Deep Dive (30 minutes)

Take turns answering these questions:

**About Work/Life:**

* What project are you most excited about right now?
* Who is causing you the most stress at work/school?
* What would your ideal workday look like?

**About Personal Life:**

* What friend have you been thinking about lately?
* What hobby would you like to spend more time on?
* What book/show/movie has captured your attention?

**About Feelings:**

* What has been keeping you up at night?
* What are you most proud of this week?
* What do you wish I understood better about you?

| ------ | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| | | |
| | | |
| | | |

**Review:** At day's end, discuss:

* What bids did you notice?
* Which responses built connection?
* Where could you have turned toward instead of away?

### The Stress-Reducing Conversation Guide

**Before starting:**

* Set aside 20 minutes
* Remove distractions (phones, TV)
* Sit facing each other

**Speaker Guidelines:**

* Share about external stress (not relationship problems)
* Be specific about events and feelings
* Allow yourself to be vulnerable

**Listener Guidelines:**

* Do NOT offer advice unless asked
* Ask questions to understand better
* Express empathy: "That sounds hard" or "I can see why you're upset"
* Take their side: "Your boss was out of line" (even if you might disagree)
* Show physical affection

**Switch roles after 10 minutes.**

## Accepting Influence Exercises (接受配偶影响练习)

### Influence Audit

Think of three recent disagreements. For each, answer:

1. What was your initial reaction?
2. Did you find something valid in your partner's view?
3. What phrase could you have used to show acceptance?

**Practice phrases:**

* "You have a point about..."
* "I can see why you'd feel..."
* "Let's find a solution that works for both of us"
* "I hadn't thought of it that way"

### The "Yes, And..." Exercise

Practice building on your partner's ideas instead of shutting them down:

* Partner A: "I think we should plan a weekend getaway"
* Partner B: "Yes, and we could make it a surprise for the kids too"

NOT:

* Partner B: "We can't afford that right now"

**Practice for 10 minutes** on various topics (vacation, home improvements, weekend plans).

## Conflict Resolution Exercises (冲突解决练习)

### Gentle Start-Up Practice

Rewrite these harsh start-ups into gentle ones:

| Harsh Start-Up | Gentle Start-Up |
| ---------------- | ----------------- |
| "You never help with the kids!" | |
| "You're so selfish!" | |
| "You always ignore me!" | |
| "You don't care about this family!" | |

**Formula:** "I feel \[emotion] when \[specific situation]. I need \[specific request]."

### The 20-Minute Break Protocol

When feeling flooded during conflict:

1. **Signal:** "I'm feeling overwhelmed. I need 20 minutes to calm down."
2. **Agree:** Set a time to resume the discussion
3. **Self-soothe:**
   * Close your eyes
   * Breathe deeply (4 counts in, 6 counts out)
   * Tense and relax each muscle group
   * Visualize a peaceful place
4. **Avoid:**
   * Thinking about why you're right
   * Rehearsing your comeback
   * Checking work emails
5. **Return:** Resume discussion when calm

### Circle Exercise for Compromise

**Step 1:** Draw two concentric circles

**Step 2:** In the INNER circle, write your non-negotiable needs

**Step 3:** In the OUTER circle (between the rings), write areas where you can be flexible

**Step 4:** Share with your partner and look for overlap

**Example (Vacation planning):**

* Inner: Must have beach access, can't be more than 4 hours flight
* Outer: Flexible on dates, hotel vs. rental, length of stay

## Overcoming Gridlock Exercises (化解僵局练习)

### Dream Detective Worksheet

Choose a recurring conflict and complete:

**The Surface Issue:** \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

**My Position:** \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

**Questions to explore my dream:**

1. What does this position represent to me?
2. What past experiences shaped this view?
3. What am I afraid will happen if I don't hold this position?
4. What positive outcome am I hoping for?
5. What personal value does this connect to?

**My Underlying Dream:** \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

### Dream Sharing Session (30 minutes)

**Setup:**

* Choose one gridlocked issue
* Set a timer for 15 minutes per person
* Speaker shares their dream without interruption
* Listener takes notes and asks only clarifying questions

**Speaker Prompts:**

* "This issue matters to me because..."
* "When I was growing up, I learned that..."
* "What I really want is..."
* "I'm afraid that if we don't..."

**Listener Guidelines:**

* Maintain eye contact
* Nod and use affirming sounds
* Ask: "Can you tell me more about that?"
* Do NOT problem-solve or defend your position
* Do NOT say "but" or "however"

**After both share:**

* Express appreciation: "Thank you for sharing that with me"
* Acknowledge the difficulty: "I know that was hard to talk about"
* Validate: "I understand better now why this matters to you"

### Family Culture Discussion

**Roles (30-minute discussion):**

* What did you learn about being a partner from your parents?
* What do you want to do the same?
* What do you want to do differently?

**Goals (30-minute discussion):**

* What is your personal life mission?

* What does "home" mean to each of you?
* What family stories are you proud of?

### Weekly Date Planning Template

**Schedule:** 2 hours, same time each week

**Rules:**

* No discussing problems or logistics
* No phones
* Focus on enjoying each other

**Activity Ideas:**

* Try a new restaurant
* Take a walk in a new neighborhood
* Attend a class together
* Visit a museum
* Cook a new recipe together
* Play a game
* Watch a movie and discuss it

**Reflection Questions (after the date):**

* What did you enjoy most?
* What did you learn about your partner?
* How can we make next week's date even better?

**Scoring:**

* 50-60: Strong foundation - maintain with daily practices
* 40-49: Good foundation - focus on 2 lowest areas
* Below 40: Needs attention - prioritize daily 5-hour investment

### Warning Signs Checklist (警告信号检查表)

Check all that apply to your relationship currently (当前关系中出现的情况):

* \[ ] **Harsh Startup** (苛刻的开始): Conversations often start with criticism or sarcasm
* \[ ] **Four Horsemen** (末日四骑士): Criticism (批评), contempt (鄙视), defensiveness (辩护), or stonewalling (冷战) appear during conflicts
* \[ ] **Emotional Flooding** (情绪淹没): I feel emotionally overwhelmed during disagreements
* \[ ] **Physiological Arousal** (生理应激): My heart races and I can't think clearly during arguments
* \[ ] **Failed Repair Attempts** (修复尝试失败): When I try to calm things down, my partner doesn't respond
* \[ ] **Negative History Rewrite** (糟糕的回忆): I have mostly negative memories of our relationship

**If you checked 2 or more:** Prioritize immediate intervention using the 7 principles.
